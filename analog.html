Fountain Pens

I started using fountain pens before having pain in my wrists but I think that introducing them here does fit the topic. You might be asking yourself why one would choose fountain pens as their writing instrument. Aren't they just a hassle with no real benefits?
Well, it depends. Of course, buying a cheap ballpoint pen is pretty straightforward and you do not have to give it a second thought. A fountain pen, on the other hand, is at least a little more expensive (though there are pretty inexpensive ones) but you still have to get ink, refill them, clean them etc. Why bother then? Well, with a fine tuned fountain pen, the pressure you need to write with it is very small. A lot smaller, in fact, than what you would need with a ballpoint pen. A nicely tuned fountain pen can write just under its own weight alone. If you are writing for a longer period this can seriously decrease the strain put on your wrists.
There are other reasons, however, that allured me to fountain pens. The most important one: writing with one feels fantastic! Also, there is a huge variety out there. Minimal, luxurious, colorful, cheap, expensive, whatever you want. The same goes for ink colors. There is huge variety in ink colors and for an introduction to the world of fountain pens I can highly recommend Goulet Pens. They have a lot of information available, videos, everything. They also have great customer service.

Bullet Journal

The Bullet Journal is an analog method for organising written content that was created/popularised by Ryder Carroll. I would describe it as a framework for designing an analog workflow to achieve whatever you want to achieve in an analog format. This could be a calender, a to do list, whatever. I use it to keey track of what I need to do for work, to keep track of stuff I am waiting for (like the answer to an e-mail), meeting notes, etc. Why use an analog tool for this when there are great digital tools? I tend to remember stuff better when writing it down manually then when I enter it into software. The same goes for brainstorming, planning, etc.
